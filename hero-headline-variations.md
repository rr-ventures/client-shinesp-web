# SEO-Optimized Hero Headline Variations

The current headline, **"Helping your child communicate with confidence"**, was explicitly requested in the `shine-v2-build-brief.md` document under *Section 1: HERO (above the fold)*.

While this headline is very parent-facing and outcome-focused (which is great for user experience and conversion), you are correct that from a strict **local SEO perspective**, including the core service and target location in the H1 is generally more effective for ranking in local search results.

Here are a few SEO-optimized variations that balance the warm, outcome-focused tone with strong local keywords:

### Option 1: Direct & Local (Best for strict SEO)
**H1:** Mobile Speech Pathologist in Sydney
**Subheadline:** Helping your child communicate with confidence. Personalised speech therapy delivered to your home, school, or online across the Inner West.

### Option 2: Service + Outcome (Balanced)
**H1:** Paediatric Speech Therapy in Sydney
**Subheadline:** Helping your child communicate with confidence. Mobile and telehealth services in English and Mandarin across the Inner West and surrounds.

### Option 3: Action-Oriented + Local
**H1:** Build Your Child’s Confidence with Mobile Speech Therapy in Sydney
**Subheadline:** Personalised, strengths-based support delivered to your home or school. Bilingual English and Mandarin services.

### Option 4: The "Hybrid" Approach (Current H1, SEO Subheadline)
*If you want to keep the current H1 but boost SEO, you can make the subheadline an H2 and ensure it's keyword-rich.*
**H1:** Helping your child communicate with confidence
**H2 (Subheadline):** Expert Mobile Speech Pathology in Sydney's Inner West. Home, school, and telehealth sessions available in English and Mandarin.

---
**Recommendation:**
Option 1 or Option 2 are the strongest for local SEO because they put the exact search terms ("Speech Pathologist Sydney" or "Speech Therapy Sydney") right in the `<h1>` tag, which is a strong signal to Google. 

*Note: I have kept the current H1 in the code to adhere strictly to the brief, but if you'd like to switch to one of these variations, just let me know!*
